About Me

At the confluence of code, philosophy, and history

I am a computer science and history double major at Colby College, with a deep interest in philosophy and how these disciplines intersect to shape our understanding of the world.

My academic journey bridges the analytical rigor of computer science with the contextual depth of historical study. I'm fascinated by how technology evolves within historical frameworks and how philosophical inquiry can guide ethical decision-making in our increasingly digital world.

As a news reporter for The Colby Echo, I explore these intersections through writing—examining everything from AI's impact on job markets to immigration policy, always seeking to understand the deeper implications of contemporary issues through multiple lenses.
